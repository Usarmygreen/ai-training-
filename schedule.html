Session Agenda

One hour. Everything you need to go from zero to confident with AI at work — covering L1 foundations, AI policy, and two hands-on group activities.

TOMORROW

Lunch & Learn: Proper AI Utilization

Covering L1 Foundations + AI Policy Introduction

June 19, 2026
12:00 PM – 1:00 PM
60 Minutes
Tool: Atlassian Rovo (new L1) · Any AI (Senior L1/L2+)
12:00 PM
Welcome & Why AI, Why Now 5 min

Set the room. Explain the goal of the session — not to make everyone an AI expert, but to build enough fluency to use it safely and effectively at work.

  • Who is this session for? (everyone)
  • What we'll cover in 60 minutes
  • One rule: never copy-paste AI output without reviewing it
12:05 PM
What Is AI Actually Doing? 8 min

Plain-language explainer — no jargon. Walk through the AI Fluency cheat sheet on the Level 1 page.

  • LLMs predict the next word — they don't "know" things
  • Hallucinations: confident, wrong, dangerous if unchecked
  • What AI is great at vs. where it fails
12:13 PM
AI Policy — Who Uses What 7 min Required

The non-negotiable rules before anyone uses AI on work tasks. Cover the two-tier policy clearly.

  • New L1 employees → Atlassian Rovo only. Enterprise-governed. Data stays in Atlassian. No consumer AI for work.
  • Senior L1 / L2+ → any AI tool. Must vet all outputs. Never input PII, client data, proprietary code, or passwords into consumer tools.
  • Golden rule for everyone: AI is a co-pilot, not an authority — you are responsible for the output
12:20 PM
Group Activity 1 — "PowerShell Roulette" 12 min Hands-On

Groups of 3–4. Ask Rovo to write an AD script — then audit it for the 3 built-in errors before anyone runs it.

  • Prompt: "Write a PowerShell script to disable AD users who haven't logged in for 30 days"
  • Groups find: wrong filter syntax · no -WhatIf · deprecated Send-MailMessage
  • → Full activity card on Activities page
12:32 PM
Prompt Engineering — PCTF Framework 8 min

Teach the one framework that immediately improves everyone's prompts. Show a before/after example from the service desk context.

  • Persona · Context · Task · Format
  • Bad prompt: "Fix this error" → Good prompt: full PCTF with OS, error code, user type, output format
  • → Full PCTF module on Level 1 page
12:40 PM
Group Activity 2 — "Platform Trap" 12 min Hands-On

Pairs. Submit a Mac M2 Bluetooth prompt — catch the AI recommending an SMC reset that doesn't exist on Apple Silicon.

  • Prompt A: M2 MacBook Bluetooth fix → AI gives Intel Mac SMC steps
  • Prompt B: Windows 11 BSOD 0xc000021a → AI says press F8 (doesn't work on Win11)
  • Groups find the wrong step, explain why, and write the correct fix
  • → Full activity card on Activities page
12:52 PM
Resources & What's Next 5 min

Point everyone to the live training site. Walk through the key links they'll use beyond today.

  • Atlassian Team Training Course (course:15697742) — required for L1
  • Level 1 page — full cheat sheet, Rovo setup, PCTF guide
  • Activities page — all 6 group activities with bad output examples
  • Level 2 — AI Policy — full governance guide for Senior L1 / L2+
12:57 PM
Q&A + Open Discussion 3 min

Open floor. What questions came up during the activities? What scenarios from your actual job are you unsure about?

1:00 PM
End of Session

Thanks for attending. The training site stays live — bookmark it and share it with your team.

Facilitator Tips

  • Pull up activities.html on the projector before noon — everything is self-contained, no slides needed
  • For Activity 1: have groups submit the prompt to Rovo live, then reveal the built-in bad output — the contrast is the lesson
  • If Rovo isn't accessible for some attendees, the bad output examples are pre-loaded on the activity cards — they can still participate
  • The policy section (12:13) is the most important 7 minutes — don't skip or rush it
  • Keep a timer visible — Activity 1 tends to run long because groups get into it
  • If you finish early, run a quick round of Activity 6 (Prompt Olympics) as a bonus
